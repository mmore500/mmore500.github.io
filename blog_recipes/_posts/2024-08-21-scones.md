title: "Scones"
date: 2024-08-21
recipe:
  servings: 8 scones
  prep: 40 min.
  cook: 20 min.
  preheat: 400 F
  source_publisher: Love and Lemons
  source_url: https://www.loveandlemons.com/scones-recipe/
  ingredients_markdown: |-
    * 2 c. all-purpose flour, spooned and leveled
    * 1/4 c. cane sugar
    * 2 tsp. baking powder
    * 1/2 tsp. sea salt
    * 8 tbsp. unsalted butter (1 stick), frozen for at least 1 hour
    * 1/2 to 1 1/2 c. mix-ins (berries, dried fruit, or chocolate chips, optional)
    * 1/2 c. cold buttermilk or heavy cream, plus more for brushing
    * 1 large egg
    * 1 tsp. vanilla extract (optional)
    * coarse sugar for sprinkling

  directions_markdown: |-
    1. line a large baking sheet with parchment paper
    2. whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt in a large bowl
    3. grate frozen butter on a box grater's large holes
    4. add grated butter to flour mixture and toss to coat
    5. use hands to work butter into flour until it resembles coarse meal
    6. add desired mix-ins and toss
    7. whisk buttermilk, egg, and vanilla together in a medium bowl
    8. pour over flour mixture and mix with spatula until shaggy dough forms
    9. knead with hands to incorporate remaining dry flour
       * dough should feel soft but not sticky
    10. form dough into a ball and transfer to lightly floured surface
    11. shape into a 7 to 8 inch disk about 1 inch thick
    12. slice into 8 equal wedges
    13. transfer to prepared baking sheet
    14. freeze scones for 15 minutes
    15. preheat oven to 400 F while scones freeze
    16. remove from freezer, brush with buttermilk
    17. sprinkle with coarse sugar
    18. bake 18 to 27 minutes until golden brown
    19. transfer to wire rack to cool

  notes_markdown: |-
    Freezing butter and the formed scones helps create tall, flaky layers. For extra flakiness, laminate the dough: fold in thirds like a letter, flatten, fold in thirds again the other direction.
