title: "Milk Bar Pie"
date: 2024-05-30
recipe:
  servings: 2 pies (10-inch)
  prep: 1 hr.
  cook: 25 min.
  preheat: 350 F
  source_name: Christina Tosi
  source_publisher: Momofuku Milk Bar
  source_url: https://www.wellseasonedstudio.com/milk-bars-crack-pie/
  ingredients_markdown: |-
    ## oat cookie:

    * 8 tbsp. unsalted butter, room temperature
    * 1/3 c. light brown sugar
    * 3 tbsp. granulated sugar
    * 1 egg yolk
    * 1/2 c. flour
    * 1 1/2 c. old-fashioned rolled oats
    * 1/8 tsp. baking powder
    * pinch baking soda
    * 1/2 tsp. kosher salt

    ## crust:

    * 1 recipe oat cookie, cooled
    * 1 tbsp. light brown sugar
    * 1/4 tsp. salt
    * 4 tbsp. melted butter

    ## filling:

    * 1 1/2 c. granulated sugar
    * 3/4 c. light brown sugar
    * 1/4 c. milk powder
    * 1/4 c. corn powder (optional)
    * 1 1/2 tsp. kosher salt
    * 16 tbsp. melted unsalted butter
    * 3/4 c. heavy cream
    * 1/2 tsp. pure vanilla extract
    * 8 egg yolks

  directions_markdown: |-
    ## oat cookie:

    1. preheat oven to 350 F
    2. cream butter with sugars on medium-high for 2-3 minutes until fluffy
    3. add egg yolk, beat 1-2 minutes until pale
    4. mix in flour, oats, baking powder, baking soda, and salt on low speed for 1 minute
    5. spread 1/4-inch thick on parchment-lined baking sheet
    6. bake 15 minutes until caramelized and set
    7. cool completely before using

    ## crust:

    1. pulse cooled oat cookie with 1 tbsp. brown sugar and 1/4 tsp. salt in food processor until wet sand texture
    2. add 4 tbsp. melted butter, knead until moisturized into a ball
    3. divide between 2 pie tins
    4. press firmly into bottom and sides

    ## filling:

    1. use stand mixer with paddle attachment (do not use hand mixer)
    2. mix granulated sugar, brown sugar, milk powder, corn powder, and salt on low speed until blended
    3. add melted butter, paddle 2-3 minutes
    4. add heavy cream and vanilla, mix 2-3 minutes until streaks disappear
    5. add egg yolks, paddle until glossy and homogenous
       * keep mixer on low speed throughout to avoid incorporating air

    ## baking:

    1. pour filling three-quarters full into crusts
    2. bake at 350 F for 15 minutes until edges are golden but center is still jiggly
    3. reduce temperature to 325 F, bake 5 more minutes
    4. cool to room temperature
    5. wrap in foil and freeze at least 3 hours or overnight
    6. move to refrigerator 1 hour before serving
    7. dust with confectioner's sugar before serving

  notes_markdown: |-
    Freezing is essential for the proper dense, gooey texture. Store in refrigerator up to 7 days or freezer up to 1 month.
